Why measure Progesterone?

Anovulation (having a menstrual cycle with no egg) is actually a common occurrence, affecting 1 in 10 women*.

A sure way to confirm ovulation is a rise in Progesterone level after peak fertility. Progesterone also supports implantation and elevated levels of PdG during the 7-10 day window after ovulation correlates to higher chances of a successful pregnancy.

* Source: National Institutes of Health

How many test strips are required per cycle?

The number of test strips required per cycle for fertility tracking depends on the specific fertility monitoring product or method you are using. Different products or methods may have varying recommendations or requirements.

Will inito work if I have pcos?

In general, Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a complex hormonal disorder that can affect the menstrual cycle and fertility in individuals assigned female at birth. PCOS can cause irregular or absent menstrual periods, hormonal imbalances, and ovarian cysts, which may impact fertility and the accuracy of fertility tracking methods, including at-home fertility monitoring products like Inito.

Do medications affect the results?

Certain medications can potentially affect the results of fertility tracking methods, including at-home fertility monitoring products like Inito. It is important to carefully read and follow the instructions provided by the specific product or method, as well as consult with a qualified healthcare provider or fertility specialist for personalized medical advice.

How accurate is Inito?
